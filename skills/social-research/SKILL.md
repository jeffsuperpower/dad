**1b. Record each paper** — For every paper you find (or received from the pack), confirm you have:
- Paper title
- Journal name and publication date

- Full URL (exactly as returned by WebSearch — never fabricate)

**1c. Scan trending health conversations** (skip if covered in Research Pack) — Use WebSearch to check:
- Trending health topics on TikTok, Instagram, X (last 1-2 weeks)
- Repeated questions, anxieties, or curiosities in comments

**Your paper inventory is your source of truth.** In Step 4, every New Research idea must link to a paper from this inventory. Every Protocol must reference research from this inventory. Health Education ideas citing data must link to a paper from this inventory. If you don't have a real paper for an idea, search for one or don't propose the idea.

## Key Principles

1. **Separate topics from formats** — Health insights and visual trends are different inputs
2. **Specific over vague** — Post ideas should be concrete, not generic. Each idea must contain enough detail for someone to say yes or no without asking follow-up questions.
3. **Anti-generic rule** — Protocol ideas must NOT be generic category-level concepts like "Cold & Flu Protocol" or "Stress Recovery Stack." Each must have a specific novel angle. If you can't articulate what makes it different from the 10,000 other articles on the topic, replace it. Research ideas must cite specific findings with numbers, not just topic areas.
4. **Timeliness matters** — Note why each idea is relevant THIS specific week — not just "this is always relevant"
5. **Early signals preferred** — Surface what's emerging, not what's saturated
6. **Always pause for selection** — User picks which 5 to brief
7. **Idea bank first** — Read and draw from the Social Idea Bank before generating ideas
8. **Source links required everywhere** — Every New Research idea must include the full URL (plain text, not markdown link) to the paper/study. Every Protocol must include URLs to supporting research. Health Explainer ideas must include a source URL when citing any data or empirical claims. No exceptions for research-based content.
9. **Real members only** — Testimonial ideas must reference actual members by SP-XXX ID and name from @Thalia's transcript scan. Never use generic placeholders. If no transcripts are available, skip testimonials and redistribute those slots to other formats.
10. **Decision-ready detail** — Every field in the idea template exists for a reason. Do not leave fields sparse or one-line. The "Story arc" for testimonials should be 2-3 sentences. The "What it covers" for protocols should list actual steps. The "The finding" for research should include sample size or key metric.
