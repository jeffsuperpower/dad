# The 5-Part Video Storytelling Framework

## Overview

Most video content fails because it's either:
- All hook, no payoff (clickbait energy)
- All information, no emotion (boring)
- No clear structure (rambling chaos)

This framework solves that. Use it for UGC scripts, testimonials, doctor-led content, and story-driven ads.

**Optimal Duration:** 45-60 seconds (Meta video ads)

---

## Core Structural Formats

Beyond the default 5-part structure (HOOK→CONTEXT→TENSION→PIVOT→PAYOFF), you can use these proven formats based on content type and audience awareness stage.

### Format 1: Problem → Insight → Relief

**Used by:** Alex Hormozi, education-first DTC

**What it is:** Expose a problem, reveal the missing insight, resolve with relief.

**Structure:**
```
Problem
   ↓
Insight
   ↓
Relief + CTA
```

**Example:**
- **Problem**: "I felt tired but my labs were normal."
- **Insight**: "Normal doesn't mean optimal."
- **Relief**: "This finally showed what mattered."
- **CTA**: "Stop guessing."

**Best for:**
- Problem-aware audiences
- Health, finance, performance categories

---

### Format 2: Before → After Awareness Shift

**Used by:** WHOOP, Calm, Apple-style brand ads

**What it is:** Contrast ignorance vs clarity, not bad vs good.

**Structure:**
```
Before state
      ↓
Moment of awareness
      ↓
After state + CTA
```

**Example:**
- **Before**: "I hoped I was healthy."
- **Moment**: "Then I saw my data."
- **After**: "Now I actually know."
- **CTA**: "Get clarity."

**Best for:**
- Warm traffic
- Trust and brand building

---

### Format 3: Expectation → Reversal → Truth

**Used by:** Contrarian brand positioning

**What it is:** Set up what viewer expects, then flip it.

**Structure:**
```
Expectation
     ↓
Reversal
     ↓
New truth + CTA
```

**Example:**
- **Expectation**: "I thought this was overkill."
- **Reversal**: "I was wrong."
- **Truth**: "It simplified everything."
- **CTA**: "If you hate extremes, this is for you."

**Best for:**
- Skeptical audiences
- Crowded categories

---

### Format 4: Question → Answer → Action

**Used by:** Sam Parr, newsletter and DTC ads

**What it is:** Open a loop, close it cleanly.

**Structure:**
```
Question
    ↓
Clear answer
    ↓
Action
```

**Example:**
- **Question**: "Why do I feel off if my labs are fine?"
- **Answer**: "Because they don't test this."
- **Action**: "Check the full picture."

**Best for:**
- Scroll-stopping hooks
- Education-led ads

---

### Format 5: Confession → Discovery → Change

**Used by:** Creator-native TikTok ads

**What it is:** Personal honesty creates trust, discovery creates authority.

**Structure:**
```
Confession
     ↓
Discovery
     ↓
Change + CTA
```

**Example:**
- **Confession**: "I ignored my health for years."
- **Discovery**: "One test explained everything."
- **Change**: "Now I'm proactive."
- **CTA**: "Do this early."

**Best for:**
- Creator UGC
- Vulnerability-first storytelling

---

### Format 6: PASP (Problem-Agitation-Solution-Proof)

**Used by:** UGC testimonial ads, health-tech direct response

**What it is:** Classic direct response structure that amplifies pain before offering solution, then provides proof of transformation.

**Structure:**
```
Problem (Hook)
     ↓
Agitation (Amplify pain)
     ↓
Solution (Introduce product)
     ↓
Proof (Transformation evidence)
     ↓
Closing (CTA)
```

**Timing:**
- **Problem (Hook):** 3-7 seconds - Open loop, create curiosity
- **Problem Agitation:** 8-15 seconds - Amplify emotional impact with specific health details
- **Solution:** 8-12 seconds - Introduce Superpower naturally
- **Proof:** 15-25 seconds - Testing discovery, action plan, implementation, measurable results
- **Closing:** 5-8 seconds - Clear CTA

**Hook Examples (Open Loop Style):**
✅ "I can't believe this one thing could tell me exactly why my hair was falling out. My doctor never checked my ferritin once."
✅ "I'm about to show you the number my doctor never checked is the reason I was losing my hair"
✅ "When I saw this in my test results, I could not believe three doctors missed this one number on my blood work, and it explained everything"
✅ "There's a reason your doctor says your labs are 'normal' but you feel terrible. And it's not what you think."
✅ "I found out why I was exhausted all the time, and it's something 90% of doctors don't even test for"
✅ "Being in the 'normal range' almost cost me my hair. Here's why..."
✅ "The reason you're exhausted isn't because you're a mom. But your doctor will never tell you what it actually is."
✅ "Your doctor is reading your blood work wrong. And it's making you think you're crazy."
✅ "Your hair isn't falling out because of stress. It's falling out because of this..."
✅ "I was struggling with 2pm exhaustion everyday for years. Trust me, it's not coffee you need. It's this number on your blood work."
✅ "If you're cold all the time and your doctor says you're fine, check this one biomarker"
✅ "Brain fog, hair loss, always tired? It's not motherhood. It's probably this."

**Avoid These Hooks:**
❌ "Your doctor didn't tell you this..." (generic)
❌ "The doctor's blind spot..." (generic)
❌ "I was struggling with my health..." (too vague)

**Example (Full PASP Script):**
- **Problem:** "I can't believe this one thing could tell me exactly why my hair was falling out. My doctor never checked my ferritin once."
- **Agitation:** "My hair was coming out in clumps in the shower. I was exhausted by 2pm every single day. I went to three different doctors and they all said my labs were normal."
- **Solution:** "Then I found Superpower. They test over 100 biomarkers—things my doctor never even looked at. And their care team actually reviews your results with you."
- **Proof:** "Turns out my ferritin was at 12. Technically 'normal' but way too low for optimal function. Their team created a personalized protocol. Six weeks later, my ferritin is at 60 and my energy is completely different."
- **Closing:** "If you're tired of being told you're fine when you know something's off, check out Superpower. Link in bio."

**Best for:**
- UGC testimonial content
- Health transformation stories
- Problem-aware audiences who've been dismissed by doctors
- Direct response Meta ads

## Format Selection Guide

**Use Problem → Insight → Relief when:**
- Audience is problem-aware but doesn't understand root cause
- You need to educate before converting
- Complex health/performance topics

**Use Before → After Awareness Shift when:**
- Building brand trust with warm traffic
- Focusing on emotional transformation, not just functional benefit
- Apple-style premium positioning

**Use Expectation → Reversal → Truth when:**
- Audience is skeptical or has objections
- Market is crowded with similar solutions
- You need to differentiate contrarian positioning

**Use Question → Answer → Action when:**
- Cold traffic scroll-stopping hooks
- Clear, simple value prop to communicate
- Education-led growth strategy

**Use Confession → Discovery → Change when:**
- Creator UGC or influencer partnerships
- Vulnerability builds trust with audience
- Personal transformation stories

**Default to 5-part HOOK→CONTEXT→TENSION→PIVOT→PAYOFF when:**
- Storytelling-first content
- Testimonial-based scripts
- Emotional narratives with clear arc

**Use PASP (Problem-Agitation-Solution-Proof) when:**
- UGC testimonial content with clear transformation story
- Health transformation narratives
- Direct response Meta ads targeting problem-aware audiences
- Scripts for talent who've experienced medical dismissal
- Need specific timing structure (Hook 3-7s, Agitation 8-15s, Solution 8-12s, Proof 15-25s)

## The 5-Part Structure

### 1. HOOK (0-5 seconds)

**Your job:** Stop the scroll. Create curiosity.

**What works:**
- **Open loops:** "The one thing that changed everything for me..."
- **Pattern interrupts:** "I used to think X. I was wrong."
- **Relatable pain:** "If your content feels flat, this is why."
- **Personal experience:** "Here's how I went from X to Y..."

**What doesn't work:**
- "Hey guys!" intros (nobody cares yet)
- Vague promises ("Learn this one trick!")
- Over-explaining upfront (get to the point)

**The secret:** Create an **information gap, and make it specific.**

Tell them just enough to make them curious, but not enough to satisfy. The brain hates unfinished puzzles—it'll keep watching to close the loop.

**Examples:**
- ❌ "Today let me walk you through my process for storytelling."
- ✅ "Spent 6 months making content nobody cared about. Then I figured out what was missing."

---

### 2. CONTEXT (5-15 seconds)

**Your job:** Set the scene. Make them understand why this matters.

**What works:**
- One clear setup (not a long life story)
- Visual context (show, don't just tell)
- Stakes (what was at risk? what did you care about?)

**What doesn't work:**
- Over-explaining
- Too much background
- Generic setups that could apply to anyone

**Keep it tight:** 1-2 sentences max.

**Examples:**
- ❌ "I've been working in design for years at various agencies learning about brand strategy and..."
- ✅ "Beautiful visuals. Zero engagement. Something wasn't right. I had to rethink everything."

---

### 3. TENSION (15-30 seconds)

**Your job:** Show the struggle. Make them FEEL the pain.

This is the emotional core. Without conflict, there's no story—just information.

**What works:**
- Internal struggles (doubt, fear, confusion)
- Specific obstacles (not vague "challenges")
- Relatable frustration (something they've strongly felt as well)

**What doesn't work:**
- Skipping straight to the solution (no tension = no engagement + boring)
- Generic problems ("it was hard"... What's hard?)
- Over-dramatizing (feels fake)

**The key:** Be specific. Not "I struggled." But "I rewrote that email four times and still couldn't bring myself to send it."

**Examples:**
- ❌ "Things didn't work out and I felt stuck."
- ✅ "Spent hours editing. Posted with hope. Three likes. Two were from my mom. I started questioning everything."

---

### 4. PIVOT (30-40 seconds)

**Your job:** Show what changed. The "aha" moment.

This is where the lesson starts.

**What works:**
- A clear shift (what you realized / decided / tried)
- Contrast (what you thought BEFORE vs. what you know NOW)
- Simplicity (one insight, not ten)

**What doesn't work:**
- Vague epiphanies ("I just figured it out")
- Multiple turning points (confusing)
- Skipping the emotion (why did it matter?)

**Keep it tight:** 1-2 lines. This isn't the explanation—just the pivot. You can also seamlessly weave it into the next section, which is what a lot of creators do.

**Examples:**
- ❌ "I figured out I should approach things differently and change my mindset."
- ✅ "Then it hit me: I was creating for the algorithm, not for people. That's why nobody cared."

---

### 5. PAYOFF (40-60 seconds)

**Your job:** Land the lesson. Give them something to take away.

This is where you deliver on the promise from the hook. Don't be stingy. Generously give what people have been waiting for!

**What works:**
- Clear takeaway (what should they DO or THINK differently?)
- Echo the opening (creates satisfying closure)
- Emotional resonance (inspire, reassure, challenge)

**What doesn't work:**
- Abrupt or empty endings ("So yeah, that's it!")
- Overloading with tactics (pick ONE thing and elaborate)
- Leaving the loop open (frustrating, not clever—that's clickbait)

**The key:** Make it feel complete. They should think, "That was worth watching."

**Examples:**
- ❌ "Just experiment with storytelling techniques and you'll improve over time."
- ✅ "Now before I hit post, I ask: Will this make someone stop scrolling? If I'm not sure, I rewrite. Simple as that."

## Bonus Tactics to Keep People Watching

### The 5-Second Rule

Change something at least once every 5 seconds:
- Camera angle
- Scene location
- Text on screen
- Music shift
- Speaker tone

**Why?** Attention is fragile. Variety keeps the brain engaged.

---

### Re-Hooks

Even mid-video, people are ready to scroll.

Drop a second hook to pull them back in:
- "But here's the real reason this happened..."
- "That's not even the interesting part..."
- "What I didn't expect was..."

This resets attention and keeps retention high.

---

### Cause and Effect

Stories feel empty without "because."

Show how one thing leads to another:
- I did X → Because of X, I got Y → And that led to Z

This makes your story feel inevitable and satisfying (not random).

**Examples:**
- ❌ "I made some changes. Results improved."
- ✅ "I stopped trying to sound professional. Started writing like I talk. Engagement doubled in two weeks. Turns out people prefer real over polished."

---

### Ultimate Secret Weapon: Open Loops (Use Sparingly)

Start a thought, pause, continue the story, then resolve it later.

The brain hates unfinished business—it'll keep watching.

**Example:**
"That choice almost ended my creative career..." [pause, tell backstory, resolve at the climax]

**Warning:** Don't overuse. One open loop per video max, or it feels manipulative.

## Workflow: How to Create a Video Script

### Step 1: Start with the emotion

Before you write a single word, ask:
- What do I want them to FEEL?
- Why does this story matter to THEM (not just me)?

If you can't answer that, you're not ready to script yet.

---

### Step 2: Draft the structure

Use this template:

```markdown
## Script: [Concept Name]

**HOOK (0-5s):** [Curiosity-driven opening]

**CONTEXT (5-15s):** [Quick setup, 1-2 sentences]

**TENSION (15-30s):** [The struggle, specific and relatable]

**PIVOT (30-40s):** [What changed, what you realized]

**PAYOFF (40-60s):** [The lesson, the takeaway]
```

Don't worry about polish. Just get the bones down. Sometimes context and tension can be compressed into one section.

---

### Step 3: Read it out loud

Does it sound like something you'd actually SAY?

If not, rewrite. Conversational > perfect.

---

### Step 3.5: Natural Conversational Language Rules

**Critical for UGC Scripts:** Every sentence must sound like natural spoken language, not headlines or ad copy.

**Always use proper first-person sentences with "I" as the subject:**

❌ WRONG: "Brain fog so bad I forgot my daughter's recital."
✅ CORRECT: "I forgot my daughter's recital because my brain fog was so bad."

❌ WRONG: "Hair coming out in clumps in the shower."
✅ CORRECT: "My hair was coming out in clumps in the shower."

❌ WRONG: "Exhausted by 2pm every single day."
✅ CORRECT: "I was exhausted by 2pm every single day."

❌ WRONG: "Three doctors, same answer: normal."
✅ CORRECT: "I went to three different doctors and they all said the same thing: normal."

**The Rule:** Every sentence should sound like a real person sharing their experience naturally—complete thoughts with subjects, verbs, and proper structure.

**Quality Check Before Finalizing:**
- Read every sentence aloud
- Does it sound like a real person talking to a friend?
- Does every sentence have a clear subject (usually "I" or "My")?
- Are there any staccato fragments that would sound unnatural when spoken?

---

### Step 4: Test the hook

Show just the first 5 seconds to a colleague:
- Do they want to know what happens next?
- Or do they shrug?

If it's not a "wait, what?" moment, rewrite the hook.

**The best way to test the hook:** POST! Only your retention graph will tell you exactly where your audience lost you.

---

### Step 5: Cut ruthlessly

Every word should earn its place.

If a sentence doesn't:
1. Move the story forward
2. Build emotion
3. Bring new information
4. Deliver the payoff

...delete it.

## Common Mistakes (And How to Fix Them)

### Mistake 1: Starting with "Hey guys!"

Nobody cares about pleasantries. Get to the point.

**Fix:** Lead with curiosity or conflict.

---

### Mistake 2: No clear conflict

"I had some challenges" = boring.

**Fix:** Be specific. What EXACTLY was hard? Make them feel it.

---

### Mistake 3: Rushing the Resolution/Payoff

"So yeah, try this!" = unsatisfying.

**Fix:** Echo your opening. Land the insight. Make it feel complete.

---

### Mistake 4: Too much information

You're not writing a tutorial. You're telling a story.

**Fix:** Pick ONE insight. Build the story around that.

---

### Mistake 5: No emotion

Facts don't make people care. Feelings do.

**Fix:** Show the internal struggle, not just the external events.

- Animated mascots

### Creator-Specific Script Guidelines

When creating scripts for specific creators (influencer partnerships, brand collaborations):

**Context You'll Receive:**
- Creator name and platform
- Audience demographics (age, gender, interests)
- Creator's content themes
- Audience pain points from research
- Selected angle based on creator alignment

**Adaptation Rules:**

1. **Voice Matching:**
   - Analyze creator's typical speaking style (formal, casual, technical, storytelling)
   - Match sentence length and vocabulary complexity
   - Maintain Superpower compliance while adopting creator's cadence

2. **Audience Alignment:**
   - Use pain points specific to creator's audience segment
   - Reference topics creator typically covers ("If you've been following my content on [topic]...")
   - Leverage creator's authority in their niche

3. **Authenticity Preservation:**
   - Script should feel like natural extension of creator's content
   - Avoid jarring brand voice shifts
   - Integrate Superpower naturally (not forced product placement)

**Example:**

**Generic UGC Script:**
"If your doctor says you're fine but you know something's off..."

**Creator-Specific Script (for Bryan Johnson-style optimizer):**
"I've optimized my sleep, my diet, my training protocol. But I was missing the foundation: comprehensive biomarker data. Here's what changed..."

### Testimonial-Based Script Guidelines

When creating scripts based on actual customer testimonials:

**Context You'll Receive:**
- Original testimonial transcript
- Primary pain point identified
- Emotional journey arc
- Key memorable quotes
- Selected angle

**Adaptation Rules:**

1. **Authenticity First:**
   - Preserve the emotional truth of the original testimonial
   - Use actual phrases from transcript (in quotes or paraphrased)
   - Don't exaggerate or dramatize beyond original

2. **Structure Alignment:**
   - Map testimonial journey to selected format naturally
   - For 5-part structure:
     - HOOK: Lead with most relatable pain point from testimonial
     - TENSION: Use original frustration/struggle details
     - PIVOT: The moment of discovery from testimonial
     - PAYOFF: Actual outcomes mentioned
   - For other formats: Adapt testimonial elements to format structure

3. **Quote Integration:**
   - Weave memorable quotes into Talent Script column
   - Use quotation marks for direct quotes
   - Attribute if using specific details ("One member told us...")

**Example:**

**Testimonial Excerpt:**
"My doctor kept saying 'everything's normal' but I felt awful. Superpower found my vitamin D was tanked and my thyroid was borderline. Finally someone believed me."

**Script Integration (5-part structure):**
- **HOOK:** "Your doctor says 'everything's normal.' But you feel awful."
- **TENSION:** "Mine said the same thing. For months. While I was exhausted, gaining weight, losing hair."
- **PIVOT:** "Superpower tested 100+ biomarkers. Turns out my vitamin D was critically low and my thyroid was borderline."
- **PAYOFF:** "Finally, someone believed me. And gave me data to prove it."

## Examples in Action

### Example 1: Problem-Aware Heart Health

**HOOK:** "If you're experiencing heart palpitations and your doctor says 'everything looks normal'..."

**CONTEXT:** "...it doesn't mean you're imagining it."

**TENSION:** "Standard heart tests often miss early warning signs. They're designed to catch disease, not detect subtle changes."

**PIVOT:** "At Superpower, we test over 100 biomarkers—inflammation markers, cholesterol subfractions, metabolic indicators that predict cardiovascular risk."

**PAYOFF:** "This isn't about diagnosing disease. It's about detecting early signs so you can take action. Your body isn't lying to you. Let's find out what it's trying to tell you."

**Structure Analysis:**
- Hook: Validation (stops scroll for women experiencing dismissed symptoms)
- Context: Sets up the "you're not crazy" message
- Tension: Explains the gap in standard testing
- Pivot: Introduces Superpower's solution
- Payoff: Empowerment + CTA

---

### Example 2: Family History Anxiety

**HOOK:** "Did your mom have a heart attack in her 50s?"

**CONTEXT:** "If you're wondering 'am I next?'—I get it. That fear is real."

**TENSION:** "But here's what your mom didn't have: the ability to see what was happening inside her body before symptoms appeared."

**PIVOT:** "Superpower tests over 100 biomarkers—including ApoB, Lp(a), inflammatory markers that reveal your actual cardiovascular risk profile."

**PAYOFF:** "You're not guessing anymore. You're knowing. And knowing means you can optimize based on YOUR data, not your family history. You don't have to repeat her story. You can write your own."

**Structure Analysis:**
- Hook: Direct question (targets family history anxiety)
- Context: Validates the fear
- Tension: Highlights what previous generation lacked
- Pivot: Shows what's now possible
- Payoff: Empowerment narrative with strong emotional close
