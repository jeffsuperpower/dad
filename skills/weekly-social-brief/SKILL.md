# Weekly Social Brief

## Purpose

Format a full week of produced content into a concise brief for team review. This is the final output of Sandy's five-phase workflow — designed to be pasted into Slack as-is.

All content lives in a **single Notion page** per week inside the "Weekly Social Briefing (Sandy)" parent page, organized with toggle headings per channel.

## Output Format

```markdown
# Social Content Brief — Week of [Date]

- **Instagram:** [N] feed posts + 5 stories
- **Twitter/X:** [N] posts (ready-to-ship copy)
- **Newsletter:** 1 edition — "[Subject line]"
- **Notion:** [Link to this week's page]
```

## Notion Page Structure

### Toggle title format
Each piece of content inside the Notion page uses this toggle title pattern:

```
▶## {Format Type}: {Descriptive Title}
```

Examples:
- `▶## Protocol: 6 Steps to Protect Your Brain While You Sleep`
- `▶## New Research: AI Diagnosed 130 Diseases From a Single Night of Sleep`
- `▶## Testimonial: Daniel O. — From 1 Hour to Minutes`
- `▶## Announcement: AI Doctor Launch (Founder Video)`

### What goes inside each toggle

**Instagram posts:**
- Carousel slide copy (text per slide — headline + body)
- Caption copy (separate from slides)
- No designer briefs, no visual direction, no color guidance

**Twitter/X posts:**
- Full ready-to-ship tweet copy
- Account (@superpower or @maxmarchione)
- Media note (what accompanies — video, image, link card)

**Newsletter:**
- Full newsletter draft ready to send

**Stories:**
- Copy per story frame
- CTA sticker type

### Campaign callout
If there is a campaign or launch for the week, include it at the end of the Notion page as a brief section with a link/image placeholder — not buried in notes.

## Rules

- **No Notes section.** Do not add a Notes section at the end of the brief.
- **No hashtags.** Never include hashtags in any content across any channel.
- **No designer briefs.** Do not include color, imagery, typography, or stylistic direction.
- **No video direction.** Do not include shot lists, music, or editing notes.
- **Max 1 post per day** per channel (Instagram and Twitter). No double-posting.
- Keep it scannable — the team should understand the full week in 30 seconds
- One Notion link to the week's page — all channels live inside it under toggle headings
- Include go-live dates
- Sandy generates this after pushing all content to Notion in Phase 5
