# Newsletter Core Format

One consistent format for the weekly Superpower Newsletter.

## Structure

### 1. Subject Line + Preheader
- Subject: MUST include an ACTUAL NUMBER + provocative hook
- Preheader: Short amplification (e.g., "Your doctor missed this")
- Goal: Curiosity gap that demands clicking
- Good: "212 toxins are slowly killing you" / "73% of your food is poison"
- Bad: "5 biomarkers explain fatigue" (generic) / "Sleep matters" (no number)

### 2. Opening
- Start: "Hi readers," + personal observation or reaction to NEW research
- Immediately follow with a shocking stat from recent study
- Keep conversational, not formal

### 3. Deep Dive (450-550 words)
- Single driving thesis — one clear throughline
- 3-5 sub-sections with narrative headers ("How did we get here?", "What you can do now")
- Evidence throughout with superscript citations (¹ ² ³)
- Bullet points for lists of 3+ items — SIMPLE items only (no brackets, no sub-descriptions)
- Practical takeaways near the end
- Product integration AFTER delivering value — list 3-4 relevant biomarkers, keep it simple
- NEVER ask rhetorical questions

### 4. Reading List (4 items)
- Intro: "Our team does a lot of reading. Here are some of our favorites from this week:"
- 2 relevant to article topic + 2 general interest from last 1-2 months
- Format: **Title in bold.** Punchy explainer sentence. "Read here:" followed by the full written-out URL as plain text (never a markdown hyperlink). Every item MUST include a working URL — no items without links.
- All items reviewed by Marcus before inclusion

### 4b. Campaign/Product Embed (when applicable)
When there is a campaign or product launch that week, include a brief product marketing callout near the end of the newsletter body (after the deep dive, before the reading list). This should:
- Feel natural within the newsletter's topic — connect the campaign to the content, not a random plug
- Be 2-3 sentences max
- Include a link to the product/landing page
- When no campaign is active, skip this section entirely

### 5. Sign-off
"Until next time, Max Marchione"

### 6. References (6-10 sources)
Full URLs, placed after sign-off

### 7. Alternative Subject Lines (5)
Different emotional angles, each with a number

## Ideation Output Format

When generating newsletter topic options (for Sandy's weekly ideation), lead with the Subject and Preheader — those are what the team evaluates first. Every field must be filled with enough detail for a confident yes/no decision.

```
1. **Subject:** [NUMBER + provocative hook — ready to use as-is in an email subject line]
   **Preheader:** [Short amplification — ready to use as-is]
   The story: [3-4 sentences describing the narrative arc — what opens the newsletter, what the core argument is, how it resolves, and what the reader walks away understanding]
   Key research: [Paper title + journal name + date + 1-sentence summary of the specific finding]
   Source: [Full URL to primary paper — REQUIRED. Use WebSearch to find the actual paper URL. Plain text, never markdown links.]
   Planned sections:
   - [Section 1 header] — [What this section covers in 1 sentence]
   - [Section 2 header] — [What this section covers]
   - [Section 3 header] — [What this section covers]
   - [Product tie-in header] — [Which biomarkers/panels connect and why]
   Superpower connection: [Specific panels, biomarkers, or member stats that tie in]
```

**RULES:**
- Lead with **Subject + Preheader**, not a topic title. The subject line is what the team needs to see first.
- The Subject line must include an actual NUMBER and be ready to use as-is — no placeholder text.
- The Preheader must be ready to use as-is — short, punchy, amplifies curiosity.
- The Source URL is MANDATORY for any research-based topic. Use WebSearch to find the real paper. If you can't find a paper, don't propose the topic.
- All URLs must be plain text, never markdown links.

### 8. Disclaimer (required)
"This email contains general information about health and wellness practices. It is not intended as medical advice and should not be treated as such. Please consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new health regimen. The information in this email is provided without any representations or warranties, express or implied."

## Writing Rules

- **Voice:** First-person, conversational, evidence-obsessed
- **Paragraphs:** 1-3 sentences max, designed for mobile
- **Sentences:** 8-15 words average, one idea per sentence
- **Lists:** Bullet points only, simple items (2-7 words), no brackets
- **Citations:** Superscript inline (¹ ² ³), full URLs in References
- **Formatting:** Bold for headers/key terms, no italic, no numbered lists, minimal emoji
- **Density:** Every paragraph must contain a data point or insight. No filler.
- **Audience:** Health-conscious adults (men and women), US-based, gender-neutral default
