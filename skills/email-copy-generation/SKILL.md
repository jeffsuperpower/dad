# The 8 Email Frameworks

Every email must follow a proven framework. Match the framework to the funnel stage and email type.

## Framework 1: AIDA (Awareness → Interest → Desire → Action)

### When to Use
**Best for:** Product-aware stage (they know about Superpower, need final push to convert)

**Email type:** Promotional

**Why it works:** Classic direct response structure. Moves from attention-grabbing hook → interesting mechanism → desirable outcome → clear action.

### Structure

**Awareness (Hook):**
- Start with attention-grabbing hook
- Problem statement, curiosity gap, or bold claim
- 1-3 sentences

**Interest (Mechanism):**
- Explain how it works or what makes it unique
- Introduce Superpower as the solution
- 2-4 sentences

**Desire (Outcome):**
- Paint the after-state (what life looks like when they solve the problem)
- Emotional payoff, not just functional benefits
- 2-4 sentences

**Action (CTA):**
- Clear, low-friction call to action
- Primary button + optional secondary link
- 1-2 sentences

### Example

**Subject:** "Your annual physical only checks 20 biomarkers. We check 100+."

**Body:**

```
[AWARENESS - Hook]
Your doctor says you're "fine."

But you're exhausted by 2pm. Your brain feels foggy. Something is off.

[INTEREST - Mechanism]
Here's the problem: your annual physical only tests 12-20 biomarkers.

That's like checking your car's oil and calling it a full inspection.

Superpower tests 100+ biomarkers in one blood draw.

We check what your doctor misses:
✅ Thyroid function (all 5 markers, not just TSH)
✅ Vitamin D, B12, magnesium
✅ Inflammation markers
✅ Metabolic health indicators

[DESIRE - Outcome]
Imagine knowing exactly why you're tired.

Not guessing. Not Googling at 2am. Knowing.

Then getting a personalized protocol from our care team. On-demand access when you have questions. And a marketplace to take action—supplements at up to 20% off, prescriptions at discounted prices.

[ACTION - CTA]
See what you're missing →
[Button: Get Started - $199/year]

Questions? Our team is here: [Link to FAQ]
```

**Best for:** Problem-aware stage (they know they have a problem but don't know solutions yet)

**Email type:** Promotional

**Why it works:** Validates pain, amplifies urgency, then provides relief. Creates emotional tension that demands resolution.

### Structure

**Problem:**
- State the pain point they're experiencing
- Make it specific and relatable
- 2-3 sentences

**Agitate:**
- Make it worse (show consequences, validate frustration)
- Build emotional tension
- 3-5 sentences

**Solution:**
- Introduce Superpower as the answer
- Show how it solves the specific problem
- Include mechanism + outcome + CTA
- 4-6 sentences

**Before (Current State):**
- Describe their current frustration/uncertainty/pain
- Be specific and empathetic
- 2-3 sentences

**After (Desired State):**
- Paint vivid picture of life after solving the problem
- Focus on emotional transformation, not just functional benefits
- 2-4 sentences

**Bridge (How to Get There):**
- Show how Superpower bridges the gap
- Step-by-step explanation or mechanism
- Include pricing + CTA
- 4-6 sentences

- Build credibility and trust

- Create reason to act now
- 2-3 sentences

## Framework 6: Quest (Question → Understand → Educate → Stimulate → Transition)

### When to Use
**Best for:** Educational/nurture emails (building trust before asking for sale)

**Email type:** Nurture

**Why it works:** Leads with curiosity (question), shows empathy (understand), delivers value (educate), creates desire (stimulate), soft ask (transition). Perfect for relationship-building.

### Structure

**Question:**
- Pose a question they've been asking themselves
- Creates open loop (curiosity gap)
- 2-3 sentences

**Understand:**
- Show you understand their situation
- Validate their frustration or confusion
- 2-3 sentences

**Educate:**
- Teach them something valuable (mechanism, data, insight)
- Deliver real value (not just sales pitch)
- 4-6 sentences

**Stimulate:**
- Create desire to act on what they learned
- Connect education to outcome
- 2-3 sentences

**Transition:**
- Soft CTA to next step
- Optional action, not required
- 1-2 sentences

### Example

**Subject:** "Why does my doctor only test TSH for thyroid function?"

**Body:**

```
[QUESTION]
You told your doctor you're exhausted, gaining weight, and your hair is thinning.

They tested your TSH (thyroid stimulating hormone). It came back "normal."

Case closed.

But you still feel terrible. Why?

[UNDERSTAND]
Here's the problem: TSH is just one marker of thyroid function.

It's like checking your car's speedometer and declaring the engine healthy.

[EDUCATE]
A comprehensive thyroid panel includes:
- TSH (thyroid stimulating hormone)
- Free T3 (active thyroid hormone)
- Free T4 (inactive thyroid hormone that converts to T3)
- Reverse T3 (inactive form that blocks T3)
- Thyroid antibodies (TPO, TG - autoimmune markers)

Your TSH can be "normal" while your Free T3 is low (causing fatigue, weight gain, brain fog).

Most annual physicals only test TSH. Why? Because it's cheap and insurance often won't cover more unless TSH is abnormal.

[STIMULATE]
But what if you could test all 5 markers—plus 95 other biomarkers—in one draw?

That's what Superpower does.

[TRANSITION]
Learn more about our comprehensive panel →
[Link to biomarker list]

Not ready to test yet? That's okay. We're here when you are.
```

# Subject Line Formulas

50% of email success is getting the open. Test religiously.

## Formula 1: Curiosity Gap

**Strategy:** Tease valuable information without revealing it (creates open loop that demands closure)

**When to use:** Problem-aware or solution-aware stages

**Examples:**
1. "The one biomarker your doctor never checks"
2. "Why you're exhausted (hint: it's not sleep)"
3. "Your annual physical is missing this"
4. "The thyroid test your doctor won't order"

# Email Psychology Principles

## Principle 1: Reciprocity

**Rule:** Give value first. Ask for the sale second (or third, or fourth).

**Application in emails:**
- Nurture emails should deliver educational value without asking for anything
- Link to free resources (blog posts, guides, webinars)
- Answer questions they didn't know they should ask
- Build trust before you ask for money

**Sequence example:**
```
Email 1 (Day 0): Educational content (no ask)
Email 2 (Day 3): Case study (soft CTA)
Email 3 (Day 7): Direct offer (clear ask)
```

---

## Principle 2: Social Proof

**Rule:** People follow others like them. Show them that people like them chose Superpower.

**Application in emails:**
- Use testimonials from relatable personas (age, pain point, outcome)
- Leverage numbers (50,000+ members)
- Show diverse outcomes (fatigue, brain fog, anxiety, hormones)
- Include specific details (ages, symptoms, biomarker results)

**Example:**
```
"Meet Sarah, 33, marketing director, exhausted despite 8 hours of sleep.

Her Superpower panel revealed low vitamin D, low ferritin, and suboptimal thyroid function—none tested in her annual physical.

8 weeks later, her energy came back."
```

---

## Principle 3: Scarcity (Ethical)

**Rule:** Real scarcity creates urgency. Fake scarcity destroys trust.

**Application in emails:**
- Use only if true (limited spots, limited-time pricing, seasonal closures)
- Explain why (testing capacity, holiday closures, pricing changes)
- Don't fake it (your audience is smart)

**Examples:**
```
✅ ETHICAL: "We're pausing new members December 15 for holiday lab closures."

❌ UNETHICAL: "Only 3 spots left!" (when there's unlimited capacity)
```

---

## Principle 4: Authority

**Rule:** People trust experts. But expertise must be credible and specific.

**Application in emails:**
- Reference care team credentials (trained at leading institutions)
- Cite data (50,000+ members, 100+ biomarkers)
- Link to research or educational content
- Don't oversell (avoid "Harvard/UCLA/Stanford" language per brand guide)

**Examples:**
```
✅ CORRECT: "Our care team includes practitioners trained at leading institutions."

❌ WRONG: "Protocols co-created with Harvard, UCLA & Stanford MDs." (removed from brand guide)
```

---

## Principle 5: Consistency (Micro-Commitments)

**Rule:** Small yeses lead to big yeses. Get micro-commitments before asking for the sale.

**Application in emails:**
- Email 1: Click to read blog post (small yes)
- Email 2: Reply to survey question (medium yes)
- Email 3: Join Superpower (big yes)

**Sequence example:**
```
Email 1: "Which biomarker are you most curious about? Reply and let us know."
Email 2: "Here's why [biomarker] matters. Read the guide →"
Email 3: "Ready to test? Join 50,000+ members →"
```

---

## Principle 6: Liking (Conversational Tone)

**Rule:** People buy from people they like. Write like a knowledgeable friend, not a corporation.

**Application in emails:**
- Use "you" and "we" (not "our members" or "the company")
- Acknowledge frustrations ("You're not crazy. The system is broken.")
- Avoid jargon (explain biomarkers simply)
- Be empowering, not scary

**Examples:**
```
❌ CORPORATE: "Superpower Health's comprehensive diagnostic platform enables members to access actionable insights."

✅ CONVERSATIONAL: "We test 100+ biomarkers in one draw, then give you a personalized protocol and on-demand access to our care team."
```

# Compliance Integration

## CAN-SPAM Footer Template

**Every marketing email must include:**

```
You're receiving this email because you [signed up at superpower.com / attended our webinar / etc.].

Superpower Health, Inc.
[Physical Address]
[Privacy Policy] | [Unsubscribe]
```

**Critical elements:**
- ✅ Reason they're receiving email (consent context)
- ✅ Company name (Superpower Health, Inc.)
- ✅ Physical postal address
- ✅ Privacy policy link
- ✅ Unsubscribe link (clear, conspicuous, functional)

---

## Health Claims Language

**Same rules as Meta ads apply to email:**

**NEVER claim:**
- ❌ "Prevent disease" / "Cure" / "Diagnose" / "Treat" / "Fix"
- ❌ "Reverse aging" / "Prevent Alzheimer's" / "Cure fatigue"

**ALWAYS use:**
- ✅ "Detect early signs" / "Optimize health" / "Track biomarkers" / "May help support"

**Email-specific consideration:**
- Longer format allows more mechanism explanation (approved)
- Still NO disease claims regardless of length
- Educational content ≠ medical advice (include disclaimer if needed)

**Examples:**
```
❌ PROHIBITED: "Prevent thyroid disease with comprehensive testing"
✅ APPROVED: "Detect early signs of thyroid dysfunction with comprehensive testing"

❌ PROHIBITED: "Cure your fatigue in 8 weeks"
✅ APPROVED: "Understand why you're tired and get a personalized protocol"

❌ PROHIBITED: "Diagnose hormonal imbalances"
✅ APPROVED: "Track hormone biomarkers and get care team guidance"
```

---

## Brand Guide Verification

**Before finalizing email copy, verify:**

**✅ CORRECT language:**
- "On-demand access to Superpower's care team" (not 24/7)
- "$199/year (most states)" or "$499/year (NY & NJ)"
- "100+ biomarkers in one blood draw"
- "Personalized protocols from our care team"
- "Supplements up to 20% off, prescriptions at discounted prices"

**❌ PROHIBITED language:**
- "24/7 access" or "year-round clinical support" → Use "on-demand access"
- "Protocols co-created with Harvard, UCLA & Stanford MDs" → REMOVED 2026-01-07
- Competitor brand names (Function Health, Quest Diagnostics) → Use generic terms

**Reference:**
- Always consult `${CLAUDE_PLUGIN_ROOT}/superpower-guide.md` for latest approved language
- Consult `${CLAUDE_PLUGIN_ROOT}/tone-of-voice-guide.md` for email-specific tone (3 types), grammar rules, and formatting guidelines

## Unsubscribe Mechanism Language

**Requirements:**
- Must be clear and conspicuous
- Must honor opt-outs within 10 business days
- Can't require login or payment to unsubscribe
- Can't sell or transfer email addresses of people who opt out

**Approved unsubscribe link text:**
```
✅ "Unsubscribe"
✅ "Update email preferences"
✅ "Manage subscription"

❌ "Click here to stay subscribed" (deceptive)
❌ Unsubscribe link in same color as background (not conspicuous)
```

# Invocation Notes

**When to invoke this skill:**
- User asks to "write email copy", "create email", "draft campaign"
- Email type + funnel stage + framework have been determined
- Target persona identified

**When NOT to invoke:**
- User asks for email sequence (use `email-sequence-builder` skill instead)
- User asks for email brief/strategy (do that first, then invoke this skill)
- Transactional emails (use simple template, not creative frameworks)

**Output format:**
Always use the Complete Email Template format (subject lines, preheader, body, CTA, footer, compliance notes, performance prediction).

**Quality checklist:**
Before delivering copy, verify:
- [ ] Framework correctly applied (structure matches chosen framework)
- [ ] 5-10 subject line variations (across multiple formulas)
- [ ] Preheader text included (30-90 characters)
- [ ] Body copy uses 12-20 word sentences (not ultra-short staccato)
- [ ] Benefits list with ✅ emoji (grouped, no blank lines between items)
- [ ] CAN-SPAM footer (address, unsubscribe, privacy)
- [ ] No health claims violations (diagnose/cure/treat)
- [ ] Brand guide compliant (on-demand, approved pricing, no competitor names)
- [ ] Performance prediction included (A/B test recommendations)
