## The Foundation: Harry Dry's Three-Test Framework

**Every sentence in your ad copy must pass ALL three tests:**

### Test 1: Can I Visualize It? (Concrete vs Abstract)
Replace vague language with specific, visualizable imagery.

**Examples:**
- ❌ "Comprehensive health insights" (fails - can't visualize)
- ✅ "See exactly why you're tired with 100+ biomarker data" (passes - can picture the app, the biomarker list, the specific insight)

### Test 2: Can I Falsify It? (Provable vs Hyperbolic)
Make provable claims backed by data, not subjective assertions.

**Examples:**
- ❌ "The world's best health testing" (fails - not provable)
- ✅ "100+ biomarkers tested in 1 blood draw" (passes - verifiable fact)

### Test 3: Can Nobody Else Say It? (Unique vs Generic)
Only Superpower should be able to make this claim.

**Examples:**
- ❌ "Expert team provides personalized care" (fails - any competitor could say this)
- ✅ "Most companies stop at testing. We go further." (passes - only Superpower offers diagnostics + protocols + marketplace)

**Test every sentence.** If it fails any test, rewrite until it passes all three.

## The 8-Step Copywriting Workflow

### Step 1: Generate Volume (Bleeding the Tap)

**Harry Dry's "Bleeding the Tap" technique:** Your first dozen ideas are predictable and mediocre. Quality emerges only after flushing out the obvious.

**Process:**
- Set timer for 10 minutes
- Write WITHOUT editing or judging
- Include weird angles, bad puns, unconventional approaches
- The last 5-10 will make you smile (or cringe less)

**For each ad:**
- Generate 20+ headline variations
- Generate 15+ hook variations
- Generate 10+ body copy versions

**Why this works:** You're forcing past the obvious, conventional, boring angles to find something fresh. The algorithm rewards creative diversity in Andromeda—volume feeds that.

---

### Step 2: Apply Three-Test Framework

**Run each variation through the three tests:**
- ✅ Visualizable? (Concrete, specific imagery)
- ✅ Falsifiable? (Provable claims backed by data)
- ✅ Unique? (Only Superpower can say this)

**Cut anything that fails.**

**Example filtering:**

**Headlines generated:**
1. "Transform Your Health Today" ❌ (fails uniqueness - generic)
2. "100+ Biomarkers Tested in 1 Draw" ✅ (passes all 3)
3. "Feel Better Than Ever" ❌ (fails falsifiability - subjective)
4. "Why Am I Exhausted at 33?" ✅ (passes all 3 - visualizable problem)
5. "Stop Guessing. Start Knowing." ✅ (passes all 3 - unique positioning)

**Keep:** 2, 4, 5
**Cut:** 1, 3

---

### Step 3: Zoom In (Abstract → Concrete)

**Harry Dry's "Zoom-In Technique":** Replace abstract concepts with specific, visualizable details by repeatedly asking "What do I actually mean here?"

**Process:**
- Draw a line on paper: "abstract" at top, "concrete" at bottom
- Rewrite the concept repeatedly, zooming in each time
- Stop when someone can close their eyes and visualize it

**Superpower Examples:**

**Abstract → Concrete (Health Insights):**
- Level 1 (abstract): "Comprehensive health insights"
- Level 2 (zooming in): "Detailed analysis of your health markers"
- Level 3 (zooming in): "Analysis of 100+ biomarkers from one blood draw"
- Level 4 (concrete): "See exactly why you're tired—your magnesium is low, cortisol is high, and vitamin D is depleted"

**Abstract → Concrete (Personalization):**
- Level 1 (abstract): "Personalized health protocols"
- Level 2 (zooming in): "Protocols customized for your body"
- Level 3 (zooming in): "Supplement recommendations based on your specific biomarkers"
- Level 4 (concrete): "Get specific supplement recommendations based on your magnesium and vitamin D levels"

**For every benefit, value prop, or claim—zoom in until it's concrete.**

---

### Step 4: Ruthless Edit (Kaplan's Law of Words)

**Harry Dry's application of Kaplan's Law:** "Any word that isn't working for you is working against you."

**Editing rules:**
- A great sentence is a good sentence made shorter
- You know copy is ready when there's nothing left to remove (Jenga tower test)
- Every word must earn its place

**Process:**
1. Remove adjectives unless backed by specifics ("comprehensive", "advanced", "personalized" → out)
2. Cut filler words ("that", "very", "really", "just", "actually")
3. Target 10-15 words per sentence maximum
4. If it can be shorter, make it shorter

**Before/After Examples:**

**Example 1:**
- ❌ Before (32 words): "Our comprehensive platform provides you with detailed health insights and personalized recommendations from our expert team of highly qualified medical professionals"
- ✅ After (8 words): "See your health data. Get actionable recommendations."

**Example 2:**
- ❌ Before (21 words): "We offer testing of over 100 different biomarkers that can help you understand what's happening inside your body"
- ✅ After (11 words): "100+ biomarkers tested. Discover exactly what's happening inside your body."

---

### Step 5: Two-Second Clarity Test

**Harry Dry's clarity standard:** Readers should grasp the main point within 2 seconds ("1 Mississippi, 2 Mississippi. You get it?")

**Application for Meta:**
- Users scroll FAST on mobile
- If headline/hook isn't immediately clear, they're gone
- Test: Can someone skim and get the promise in 2 seconds?

**Testing process:**
1. Show headline/hook to someone for 2 seconds
2. Take it away
3. Ask: "What's the promise?"
4. If they can't answer → rewrite

**Examples:**

**Fails 2-second test:**
- ❌ "Leverage comprehensive biomarker data with clinical protocols to optimize your health outcomes"
- Why: Too many concepts, vague language, requires processing time

**Passes 2-second test:**
- ✅ "Discover why you're tired. Then fix it."
- Why: Simple problem → simple solution, immediately clear

- ✅ "Your doctor tested 12 markers. We test 100+."
- Why: Clear comparison, specific numbers, obvious benefit

### Step 7: Uniqueness Audit (Competitive Moat)

**Harry Dry's uniqueness test:** Could a competitor sign your exact copy? If yes, it's not differentiated enough.

- ❌ "Advanced technology"

### Step 8: Format for Medium (Design + Copy Together)

**Harry Dry's principle:** Don't write copy separately from design. Consider the medium while writing.

**For Meta Ads (Mobile-First):**

**Line length:**
- 5-10 words per line (mobile readability)
- Use line breaks frequently
- Don't cram multiple ideas in one sentence

**Visual hierarchy:**
- Blank lines between sentences (generous white space)
- Emoji for benefits (✅ grouped together, no blank lines between them)
- Short paragraphs (1-2 sentences max)

**Formatting while drafting:**

**Example (formatted for mobile):**
```
Why am I exhausted at 33?

Your doctor tested 12 markers.

Said you're "fine."

We test 100+ biomarkers in one draw.

Then show you exactly what's off.

✅ 100+ biomarkers (vs typical 12-20)
✅ Results in 5-10 days with protocols
✅ On-demand access to care team

Only $199/year.

Join at superpower.com.
```

**NOT like this (desktop-first, dense):**
```
Why am I exhausted at 33? Your doctor tested 12 markers and said you're "fine." We test 100+ biomarkers in one draw, then show you exactly what's off. 100+ biomarkers (vs typical 12-20), results in 5-10 days with protocols, on-demand access to care team. Only $199/year. Join at superpower.com.
```

**Formatting checklist:**
- ✅ Short lines (5-10 words per line)
- ✅ Generous white space (blank lines between key ideas)
- ✅ Benefits grouped with ✅ emoji
- ✅ Easy to scan on mobile
- ✅ Text overlays considered (where will "100+" appear on creative?)

## Compliance Integration

**All Harry Dry principles must respect brand compliance:**

### ✅ Approved Language (from `${CLAUDE_PLUGIN_ROOT}/superpower-guide.md`):
- "On-demand access to Superpower's care team" (NOT "24/7")
- "Personalized protocols" or "Clinical protocols"
- "Optimize your health", "Track biomarkers", "Detect early signs"
- "$199/year" (most states)
- "100+ biomarkers in 1 blood draw"
- "Supplements up to 20% off"

### ❌ Prohibited Language:
- "Prevent disease", "Cure", "Treat", "Fix", "Diagnose"
- "24/7 access" (use "on-demand" instead)
- "Harvard/UCLA/Stanford clinicians" (can mention credentials in footnotes, not main copy)
- Competitor names in ads (Function, Quest, etc. - use generic "other testing companies")

### Key Compliance Notes:
- Focus on **optimization** (approved) not **prevention** (prohibited)
- Data-driven claims must be **verifiable** from `${CLAUDE_PLUGIN_ROOT}/superpower-guide.md`
- Biomarker examples (testosterone, cortisol, inflammation) are for **illustration only**, not outcome guarantees
- Results timelines must be **realistic** ("5-10 business days" is verified, "get results in 24 hours" is not)

**Compliance workflow:**
1. Write copy using Harry Dry principles
2. Run through `${CLAUDE_PLUGIN_ROOT}/superpower-guide.md` approved language check
3. Replace any prohibited language with approved alternatives
4. Verify all data claims match `${CLAUDE_PLUGIN_ROOT}/superpower-guide.md` exactly
