# Member Email Formats

Standard layouts for Luna's 4 monthly member emails. Reference this skill during production to keep emails consistent.

---

## Shared Standards (All 4 Types)

### Subject Lines
- **Length:** 40-60 characters (optimal for mobile)
- **Style:** Conversational, not clickbaity — members trust us
- **Variants:** 3-5 per email for A/B testing
- **Rules:**
  - No ALL CAPS
  - No emojis
  - No exclamation points
  - No false urgency
  - No deceptive claims

### Preheader
- **Length:** 30-90 characters
- **Purpose:** Complements the subject line (not repeats it)
- **Style:** Adds context or curiosity to drive the open

### Greeting
- Always: `Hi [First_Name],`
- Never: "Dear Member", "Hello!", "Hey there!"

### Footer (CAN-SPAM Required)
```
You're receiving this email because you're a Superpower member.

Superpower Health, Inc.
[Physical Address from brand guide]
[Privacy Policy] | [Manage Preferences] | [Unsubscribe]
```

### General Formatting
- Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences max)
- Line breaks between paragraphs
- Bullet points for lists of 3+ items
- Bold for key phrases (sparingly)
- No emojis except checkmarks in benefit lists
- No exclamation points
- Sentence length: 10-20 words (conversational range)

## Format 1: Members-Only Newsletter

**Send:** Week 1 of each month
**Length:** 500-700 words
**Tone:** Warm insider

### Layout

```
SUBJECT: [40-60 chars — insider angle, not generic]
PREHEADER: [30-90 chars]

Hi [First_Name],

[OPENING HOOK — 1-2 sentences]
Something interesting, surprising, or personally relevant.
Sets up the topic with warmth.

[MAIN CONTENT — 3-4 paragraphs]
The core insight, story, or perspective.
Written in flowing prose, not bullet-point listicle style.
Can include data points, observations, or behind-the-scenes context.
Each paragraph advances the idea.

[SUPERPOWER TIE-IN — 1 paragraph]
How this connects to their membership.
Subtle, never salesy. "As a member, you can see this in your dashboard..."
or "This is one of the reasons we test [biomarker]..."

[SIGN-OFF]
Until next month,
The Superpower Team

---
[FOOTER]
```

### Key Principles
- Different topic from the public newsletter (check Noah/Max)
- Member-exclusive framing: insider access, first look, deeper context
- No hard sell
- Can reference their results or dashboard
- Written as a cohesive narrative, not a collection of sections

## Format 2: Product Feature

**Send:** Week 2 of each month
**Length:** 200-400 words
**Tone:** Helpful guide

### Layout

```
SUBJECT: [40-60 chars — benefit or "did you know" angle]
PREHEADER: [30-90 chars]

Hi [First_Name],

[HOOK — 1-2 sentences]
A relatable problem or scenario the feature solves.
OR: "Did you know your Superpower membership includes..."

[FEATURE INTRODUCTION — 1 paragraph]
What it is and why it matters. Clear, no jargon.

[HOW TO USE IT]
1. [Step 1]
2. [Step 2]
3. [Step 3]
(3-5 numbered steps or bullet points)

[CTA]
[Button: Try It Now →] or [Button: Explore [Feature] →]

---
[FOOTER]
```

### Two Modes

**New Launch Mode:**
- Lead with the announcement: "We just launched..."
- Explain what it does and why they'll love it
- Clear steps to try it
- Excitement without hype

**Tips & How-To Mode:**
- Lead with "Did you know?" or a problem it solves
- Surface value they might be missing
- Step-by-step instructions
- "Get more from your membership" energy

### Key Principles
- Scannable — headers, bullets, short paragraphs
- Link directly to the feature
- Include visual direction notes for the design team (screenshot placement, etc.)
- No fluff — respect their time

## Format 3: Community Spotlight

**Send:** Week 3 of each month
**Length:** 200-400 words
**Tone:** Celebratory, personal

### Layout

```
SUBJECT: [40-60 chars — member's story hook, not generic "Member Spotlight"]
PREHEADER: [30-90 chars]

Hi [First_Name],

[INTRODUCTION — 1-2 sentences]
Brief intro to the member: name, age, one-line context.
"Meet [Name], a [age]-year-old [descriptor] who..."

[THEIR STORY — 2-3 paragraphs]
What brought them to Superpower.
What they discovered.
How it changed their approach to health.
Written as a narrative, not a Q&A.

[KEY QUOTE — block quote format]
> "[Verbatim quote from their testimonial profile]"
> — [Name]

[CONNECTION — 1 paragraph]
How their story relates to the reader.
"If you've ever felt..." or "Many members share [Name]'s experience..."
Makes it personal to the reader, not just about the featured member.

[SOFT CTA]
[Link: Have your own story? We'd love to hear it →]
or
[Link: Check your [relevant biomarker] results →]

---
[FOOTER]
```

### Key Principles
- Source ALL content from the member's testimonial profile
- Verbatim quotes only — never paraphrase their words
- Check consent and usage rights before featuring
- Tell their journey, not a product pitch
- The member is the hero, not Superpower

## Format 4: Timely Protocol

**Send:** Week 4 of each month
**Length:** 300-500 words
**Tone:** Practical, timely

### Layout

```
SUBJECT: [40-60 chars — timely, actionable angle]
PREHEADER: [30-90 chars]

Hi [First_Name],

[HOOK — 1-2 sentences]
Timely observation or relatable scenario.
"If you've been [seasonal symptom] lately, you're not alone..."

[CONTEXT — 1 paragraph]
Brief explanation of why this matters RIGHT NOW.
Seasonal, cultural, or health-relevant timing.

[TIPS — numbered list]

**1. [Tip title]**
[1-2 sentence explanation of why + how]

**2. [Tip title]**
[1-2 sentence explanation of why + how]

**3. [Tip title]**
[1-2 sentence explanation of why + how]

**4. [Tip title]** (optional)
[1-2 sentence explanation of why + how]

**5. [Tip title]** (optional)
[1-2 sentence explanation of why + how]

[BIOMARKER TIE-IN — 1 paragraph]
"Your Superpower results include [relevant biomarkers].
Check your dashboard to see where you stand."
Natural connection to their membership — not forced.

[CTA]
[Button: Check Your Results →]
or
[Button: Book Your Next Test →]

---
[FOOTER]
```

### Key Principles
- Every protocol must tie to biomarkers Superpower actually tests
- Tips must be evidence-based but written accessibly
- No disease claims — use "support", "optimize", "may help"
- The value is in the TIMELINESS — this matters because of when it's sent
- 3-5 tips is the sweet spot (don't overload)

## Subject Line Style Guide (By Email Type)

### Members-Only Newsletter
Focus on curiosity and insider access:
- "Something we've been thinking about"
- "The health metric nobody talks about"
- "Members first: [topic preview]"

### Product Feature
Focus on utility and discovery:
- "A feature you might have missed"
- "3 ways to get more from your dashboard"
- "New: [feature name] is live"

### Community Spotlight
Focus on the member's story, not generic "spotlight" language:
- "[Name] spent 2 years being told she was fine"
- "What [Name] found in his first Superpower test"
- "A story that might sound familiar"

### Timely Protocol
Focus on timeliness and actionability:
- "[Season/Topic]: what to do now"
- "Your [month] health checklist"
- "[Number] things to [action] before [event/season]"
