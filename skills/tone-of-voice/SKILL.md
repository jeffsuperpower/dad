# Tone of Voice Guide

## Brand Voice

Superpower sounds like a **smart, confident friend who knows a lot about health science**. Not a doctor lecturing from a podium. Not a wellness influencer selling vibes. An intelligent companion who explains clearly and earns trust through evidence.

## Core Attributes

| Attribute | Expression |
|-----------|------------|
| **Intelligent** | Uses health/science terminology but always explains it |
| **Confident** | Takes a stance, has opinions, doesn't hedge everything |
| **Curious** | Drawn to what's new, surprising, counterintuitive |
| **Accessible** | Complex ideas made simple without dumbing down |
| **Premium** | Restrained, tasteful, never cheap or desperate |
| **Human** | Conversational, relatable, not corporate |

## Do / Don't

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Lead with feeling or identity | Over-explain or lecture |
| Speak directly to the reader ("you") | Be boring, safe, or generic |
| Make relatable analogies | Force trends or try too hard |
| Take a stance, have an opinion | Post without a clear POV |
| Use restraint — less is more | Use long-winded copy |
| Explain WHY something matters | List facts without context |
| Be specific and data-driven | Make vague wellness claims |
| Originate new thought | Recycle what's already everywhere |

## Channel Adaptations

| Channel | Tone Shift |
|---------|------------|
| **Instagram (Iris)** | Aspirational, visually-led, culturally fluent. Polished but not corporate. Emojis sparingly in captions only. |
| **Twitter/X (Tom)** | Bold, direct, punchy. More opinionated and raw. No emojis ever. Spaced line breaks. |
| **Newsletter (Noah)** | Conversational, evidence-dense, personal. First-person. Longer form, flowing prose. |

## AI Writing Giveaways (MANDATORY CHECK — All Channels)

Every piece of content must be checked against this list before delivery. These patterns instantly signal AI-written copy. If you catch yourself writing any of them, rewrite.

### The patterns

1. **Short sentences in threes.** "No pain. No warning signs. Just a biomarker climbing in the background." — The staccato triplet is the most recognizable AI rhythm. Vary your pacing. Two short sentences are fine. Three in a row is a tell.

2. **Generic insight framed as depth.** "This is the health crisis no one is tracking." / "It's a powerful reminder." / "And that changes everything." — If you can swap the sentence into any other topic and it still works, it's filler. Cut it entirely.

3. **The question-fragment-answer pivot.** "The symptoms? Fatigue. Brain fog. Low energy." / "And this approach? It's what separates..." — This is a ChatGPT fingerprint. Restructure so the point lands without the question-answer crutch. Just state it.

4. **Artificial vulnerability.** "I used to think X. Now I realise Y." — Manufactured epiphanies from an AI are dishonest. Only use genuine first-person reflection when it's from a real person.

5. **"Not because X — because Y" constructions.** AI leans on this contrast structure constantly. Rewrite to make the point directly instead of through negation.

6. **"Quietly" doing anything.** "They're quietly revolutionizing..." / "This is quietly becoming..." — If something matters, say why it matters. "Quietly" is a hedge that sounds important but says nothing.

7. **Cliche business/inspiration phrases.** "Game-changer." / "Let that sink in." / "Here's the thing." / "The future of X is here." — Content-mill filler. Replace with a specific, concrete observation.

8. **Paired dramatic adjectives at the end.** "invisible and unmonitored" / "silent and unchecked" / "hidden and untreated" — AI loves closing with two adjectives joined by "and." If you need a closer, use a concrete fact instead.

### How to self-check

Read the caption back. For every sentence, ask: "Could I swap this sentence into a post about sleep, gut health, or heart disease and it would still make sense?" If yes, the sentence is generic. Replace it with something specific to THIS data, THIS finding, THIS chart.

## Vocabulary

**Use:** detect early signs, optimize health, track biomarkers, may help support, novel insight, actionable, evidence-based
**Never use:** prevent disease, cure, diagnose, treat, fix, guaranteed results, lab tests (use "biomarkers"), marker (always use "biomarker")
